Titanic Data Set Investigation¶

1. Introduction¶

The sinking of the RMS Titanic is one of the most infamous shipwrecks in history. On April 15, 1912, during her maiden voyage, the Titanic sank after colliding with an iceberg, killing 1502 out of 2224 passengers and crew. This sensational tragedy shocked the international community and led to better safety regulations for ships.

One of the reasons that the shipwreck led to such loss of life was that there were not enough lifeboats for the passengers and crew. Although there was some element of luck involved in surviving the sinking, some groups of people were more likely to survive than others, such as women, children and the upper-class.

2. Goals of this Analysis¶

The data set overview from Kaggle suggests some factors that made people more like to survive for example Sex, Age and the class. In this analysis we will find relationships between variables, visualise the data and find answers to some interesting questions:

  1. Was the "women and children first" code of conduct really followed?
  2. Did the above depend on the class in which people were travelling? That is, women and children that survived were travelling in upper class?

  3. Does the survival of a Passenger depend on travelling alone or with family/spouse, Fare or port of embarkation?

    Let's get started and see what is in our data!

Dealing with missing values¶

Cabin has the most values missing, that is, 687/891 followed by Age with 177/891 values missing and Embarked with only 2/891 values missing.In general there are the following types of remedies for missing data:

  • Delete the samples with any missing data elements
  • Impute the value of the missing data
  • Remove a variable which has high instances of missing data,especially if there are other variables which measure similar aspects of the variable or characteristic being studied.

In this dataset,if we delete the samples with any missing data, we lose important information about a passenger held in other variables which may affect the chances of survival of a passenger for example(Sex, Pclass, Embarked etc). Also, the titanic had 2208 passengers and crew on board but we have the survival information for only 891 passengers. The collected data has a survival bias because atleast part of it was collected after the ship sank. Deleting the samples with missing values will make the dataset even smaller with more bias.

I will use Imputation and Variable removal techniques to deal with the missing values.

Processing Embarked

'Embarked' has only 2 values missing in this dataset. It makes sense to replace these values with most common port in that coloumn. In other words, the missing values are replaces by the mode of the column as it has the highest frequency of occurence.

In [57]:

Processing Age

Imputing values for missing data in 'Age' is tricky . Replacing missing values with mean age of the passengers can distort the disturibution of the Age variable as it is affected by outliers. Therefore, replacing the missing datawith median age is more sensible as it is more robust to outliers. The median age of passengers may differ on the basis of gender and class. I have grouped the data set of the basis of gender and class to identify the median age for male and female passengers in each class.

4. Conclusion¶

While the Titanic was sinking, the officers famously prioritized who was allowed in a lifeboat with the strict maritime tradition of evacuating women and children first. The statistical calculation results clearly reflect the first part of this policy as, across all classes, women were much more likely to survive than the men. We can also see that the women were younger than the men on average and were more likely to be traveling with family. Also, being a women, being a child, being in 1st class and travelling with family were all factors that could boost the chances of survival.

6. Recommendations¶

Statistical Tests

Chi-Square Test: This test is used to derive the statistical significance of relationship between two categorical variables.Chi-square is based on the difference between the expected and observed frequencies in one or more categories in the two-way table. It returns probability for the computed chi-square distribution with the degree of freedom.

Probability of 0: It indicates that both categorical variable are dependent

Probability of 1: It shows that both variables are independent.

Probability less than 0.05: It indicates that the relationship between the variables is significant at 95% confidence.
